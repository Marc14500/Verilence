How It Works
1
Ingest & chunk ▸
Document is extracted and split into logical legal sections using Legal-BERT

Your document is processed by pdfplumber for text extraction, then chunked into semantically meaningful sections — Article I, Section 2.3, etc. — rather than arbitrary character counts. Each chunk is then encoded into a 768-dimensional vector using Legal-BERT (nlpaueb/legal-bert-base-uncased), a transformer model fine-tuned specifically on legal text. These vectors are indexed in Qdrant, a vector database, enabling fast semantic search across the entire document.

Why this matters for auditability: Every chunk is traceable back to its exact source location in the original document. No content is summarized or lost.

2
Contradiction detection ▸
The contradiction engine detects conflicts across sections. Gemini 2.5 Pro synthesizes findings into plain-language explanations

Google Gemini 2.5 Pro — the same model powering Google's enterprise products — receives the full document text and a structured prompt instructing it to identify contradictions, ambiguities, and conflicting obligations. It returns structured JSON: each finding includes the two conflicting sections, verbatim quotes, the legal problem, estimated financial impact, and a raw confidence value.

We use Gemini because it is a production-grade, commercially supported model with documented performance characteristics — not an experimental or proprietary black box. You can independently verify its capabilities.

Why this matters for auditability: The exact prompt and response are logged. Every finding is sourced to specific document sections with verbatim quotes you can verify against the original.

3
Glass Box scoring ▸
Every finding gets a risk score and confidence rating with full mathematical audit trail

Each finding is scored by three independent signals, each with a disclosed weight:

SignalWhat it measuresWeight
Text clarityHow obvious is the contradiction in the document text?35%
Gemini reliabilityConfidence returned by Gemini 2.5 Pro for this finding35%
Chunk qualityDid retrieval pull the directly relevant document sections?30%

Final confidence = (Text clarity × 0.35) + (Gemini reliability × 0.35) + (Chunk quality × 0.30)

Risk is scored separately across three dimensions: financial exposure, litigation probability, and regulatory/compliance risk. The composite risk score determines escalation routing.

Why this matters for auditability: Every number on the dashboard has a formula behind it. There is no black box component. A lawyer, auditor, or regulator can trace any confidence score back to its three inputs and verify the arithmetic themselves.

4
Routed dashboard ▸
Findings routed to Senior Partner, Analyst Review, or Auto-Approve based on risk × confidence

Every finding is automatically routed using a documented decision matrix:

Risk scoreConfidenceRouting
≥ 0.70 (High)AnySenior Partner Escalation
0.40–0.69 (Medium)> 60%Analyst Review
< 0.40 (Low)> 85%Auto Approve

No finding disappears. Every routing decision is logged with the risk score, confidence score, and the specific rule that triggered it — creating a complete chain of custody from document upload to human review.

Why this matters for auditability: The routing logic is fully disclosed. If a regulator asks why a finding was escalated or approved, the answer is a documented rule, not a model's opinion.
