Ambiguous Ownership of Intellectual Property Derivatives

What We Found

Section 9.1 Work Product: 'All deliverables, materials, and work product created by the Contractor specifically for [Company Name] in the performance of services under this SOW... shall be the sole property of [Company Name].' vs Section 9.2 LLM Framework — Contractor IP: 'Any enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives of the Contractor IP developed during this engagement shall be addressed in the Second SOW or a separate IP agreement...' Problem: The contractor's core task (Section 2.1) is to create a 'strategic roadmap' and 'design and initiate use of the LLM framework as a pre-sales discovery and qualification tool'. It is ambiguous whether this 'roadmap' and 'design' constitute 'work product' owned by the company under 9.1, or 'enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives' of the contractor's IP with ownership deferred under 9.2. If it's considered 'work product', the company could own the implementation plan for the contractor's core IP, potentially cutting him out of a future deal while using his strategic plan.

What We Found
