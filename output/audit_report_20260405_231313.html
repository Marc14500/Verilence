Ambiguous Ownership of Intellectual Property Enhancements

What We Found

Section 9.1 Work Product: 'All deliverables, materials, and work product created by the Contractor specifically for [Company Name] in the performance of services under this SOW shall be considered work made for hire and shall be the sole property of [Company Name].' vs Section 9.2 LLM Framework — Contractor IP: 'Any enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives of the Contractor IP developed during this engagement shall be addressed in the Second SOW or a separate IP agreement, should the parties elect to proceed with formal integration.' Problem: A conflict exists between the company's broad claim to all 'work product' and the deferral of ownership for 'enhancements' to the contractor's pre-existing IP. The company provides resources (data, infrastructure) to develop these enhancements for its benefit (e.g., as a pre-sales tool). This work could be defined as 'work product created...specifically for' the company under 9.1. However, 9.2 states ownership of these same enhancements will be decided later. If the parties do not proceed to a Second SOW, there is no mechanism to resolve ownership, creating a high risk of an IP dispute. The contractor could lose control of improvements to their core IP, or the company could lose rights to tools developed with its resources.
