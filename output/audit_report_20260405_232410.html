Ambiguous Ownership of Derivative Intellectual Property

What We Found

Section 9.2 LLM Framework — Contractor IP: Any enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives of the Contractor IP developed during this engagement shall be addressed in the Second SOW or a separate IP agreement, should the parties elect to proceed with formal integration. vs Section 2.1 LLM Assessment Deep Dive & Strategic Roadmap: ...the Company will support the Contractor with drafting a high-level roadmap for advancing the build, inclusive of a testing strategy; the Company would provide the infrastructure, data and testing environment to complete. Problem: The SOW states that the Company will provide significant resources (infrastructure, data, testing environment) to help the Contractor develop 'enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives' of the Contractor's pre-existing IP. However, it explicitly defers determining ownership of these valuable new works to a potential future agreement. If no Second SOW is executed, there is no provision governing who owns the valuable IP created with Company resources during the trial period. This creates a high risk of a significant legal dispute over ownership of the core asset being developed.
