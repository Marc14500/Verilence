Ambiguous Ownership of Developed Intellectual Property

What We Found

Section 9.1 Work Product: All deliverables, materials, and work product created by the Contractor specifically for [Company Name] in the performance of services under this SOW shall be considered work made for hire and shall be the sole property of [Company Name]. vs Section 9.2 LLM Framework — Contractor IP: Any enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives of the Contractor IP developed during this engagement shall be addressed in the Second SOW or a separate IP agreement, should the parties elect to proceed with formal integration. Problem: The agreement defers the critical decision of who owns 'enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives' of the Contractor's IP. The core of the work involves creating a 'roadmap', 'testing strategy', and 'initial test results' (Sec 2.1) using Company resources. These items could be considered 'work product' under Section 9.1 (owned by the Company) or 'enhancements' under Section 9.2 (ownership deferred). If the parties do not execute a second SOW, there is no agreed-upon mechanism to assign ownership of the valuable IP co-developed during the trial, creating a high probability of a legal dispute.
