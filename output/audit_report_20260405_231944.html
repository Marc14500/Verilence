Deferred Ownership of Intellectual Property Derivatives

What We Found

Section 2.1 LLM Assessment Deep Dive & Strategic Roadmap: The SOW requires the Company to 'support development of the roadmap to achieve MVP' and provide 'infrastructure, data and testing environment to complete' work on the Contractor's framework. vs Section 9.2 LLM Framework — Contractor IP: 'Any enhancements, adaptations, or derivatives of the Contractor IP developed during this engagement shall be addressed in the Second SOW or a separate IP agreement, should the parties elect to proceed with formal integration.' Problem: The agreement mandates joint work on the Contractor's proprietary IP but punts the critical decision of who owns the resulting improvements ('derivatives') to a future, non-guaranteed agreement. This is an 'agreement to agree,' which is often legally unenforceable. If the relationship sours and no second SOW is signed, both parties could lay claim to the enhancements made during the trial period, leading to costly litigation. The company performed work and provided resources, but it's based on the contractor's pre-existing IP.

Incomplete Termination Clause

What We Found

Section 10. Termination: 'In the event of termination by [Company Name] without cause, the Contractor shall retain any milestone bonuses earned through the date of termination.' vs Section 10. Termination: The clause is silent on the consequences of (a) termination by the Company *for cause*, or (b) termination by the Contractor. Ambiguous Contracting Entity

What We Found

ENGAGEMENT OVERVIEW: The client is identified as 'Client / Company UtopIQ, a Schmidt-Morgan Corporation'. vs Multiple Sections (e.g., 5.2, 6.1, 7, 9.1, 12): The agreement repeatedly uses the placeholder '[Company Name]' to refer to the client, including in the signature block.
